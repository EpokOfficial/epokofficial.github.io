A chapter I’m closing.

This music comes from a time when my world was small and loud at the same time. The streets, the studio, the nights that bled into mornings. The pressure to survive, to perform, to numb, to keep moving even when nothing was changing. I lost people in that life— brothers, homies, names that don’t need to be said to be felt. Funerals became familiar. Absence became normal.

Somewhere in all of that, I lost parts of myself too. I confused loyalty with obligation. Pain with purpose. Motion with progress. I kept telling myself this was just how it was, that this was the path, that this was all there was. But slowly, and then all at once, I realized there was more to life than repeating the same cycles and calling it strength.

This music isn’t a celebration of that world. It’s a record of it. It’s what I wrote when I didn’t know how else to process grief, anger, addiction, or survival. It’s where I put the thoughts I couldn’t say out loud. In that way, it became my intervention—my diary—my way out.

I’m giving this to you because I don’t want to carry the weight anymore. What’s here belongs to the past, and I’m leaving it behind.

What you’re hearing is my life in fragments—
the pain I didn’t know how to speak,
the tears I learned to hide,
the nights I barely survived,
the thoughts that stayed locked in these records like pages in a diary.

Survive. Rise. Become.
